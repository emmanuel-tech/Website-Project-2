Codename: CAMERA BODY

There are varying companies offering different camera options for the general public. When it comes to figuring out what camera to buy, its best to know what types of cameras are available, and then narrow down what attributes and capabilities are most important to a consumer in order to differentiate on which brand to get. Let's begin by simply understanding the different types of cameras.

  • DSLR cameras and mirrorless cameras
  • Full Frame Sensors and ASP-C Sensors (Cropped Sensors)

DSLR Cameras and Mirrorless Cameras

In the simplest terms, a DSLR camera incorporates a mirror in front of the camera sensor, and utilizes an optical viewfinder. A mirrorless camera has an exposed sensor (the sensor can be seen when the camera lens is removed), and since there is no mirror, no viewfinder is required within the camera. Due to a lesser amount of components, mirrorless cameras are typically more lightweight than DSLR cameras, as well as more compact.

Full Frame Sensors and ASP-C Sensors (Cropped Sensors)

To place this in simplest terms, imagine a camera having a piece of technology called a sensor that captures an image. Older camera models had sensors measuring 35mm in size, which is a measure that persists till today. Cameras classified as full-frame cameras specify that that camera wields a 35mm sensor camera. An APS-C camera (otherwise known as a cropped sensor camera) wield a smaller sensor than a full frame sensor. To compare the image captured by full frame cameras versus APS-C cameras, imagine this. If you have a 500 by 1000 unit scene, a full frame may capture the entire scene, the full 500 by 1000 units. An APS-C sensor camera would capture a small portion of that 500 by 1000 unit scene; so while the full frame camera can capture 500 by 1000 units of the scene, an APS-C may capture a view of the scene measurable to 300 by 600 units. Full frame cameras tend to be more expensive, higher in quality, and used for professional purposes. APS-C cameras provide adequate quality, are more affordable than full frame cameras, and are used for multiple purposes.
