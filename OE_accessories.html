Codename: ACCESSORIES

Photography and filmmaking incorporates an amplitude of equipment to capture the best possible images under any type of circumstance or scenario. This page will describe varying tools, accessories, and rigs one might need in order to have successful shoots. The tools include:

  • Spare batteries
  • Memory cards
  • Tripod/monopod
  • Slider
  • Battery Packs
  • Camera body cages
  • Carrying methods

Spare batteries

All, if not most, cameras come packaged with one battery for use. Typically shoots can run anywhere from a few minutes to hours to full days at a time. Having batteries ensures that footage can continuously be captured even when the active battery in the camera depletes of energy.

Memory cards

Memory cards are the storage hardware of all photos and videos taken by a camera. Memory cards range from storying a few GBs of media to large amounts of media. Having spare memory cards allows the continuous storing of necessary media.

Tripod/monopod

Tripod and monopods are stands that can hold cameras in varying locations. A camera is mounted on a tripod or monopod, holds the camera, and responds to the desires of the camera operator.

Slider

A slider allows smooth side to side or front to back movement depending on how the camera is mounted, permitting a camera to capture a scene dynamically. Sliders can be placed on the ground or mounted on tripods, allowing for elevation in footage.

Battery Packs

Battery packs charge batteries, some charging one battery at a time versus others charging multiple batteries simultaneously.

Camera body cages

Camera body cages are metal frames form-fitted for the respective camera they are meant to fit. Cage bodies have multiple threads, holes, and inserts that allow accessories to be attached to the frame, essentially allowing accessories to attach to the camera. Some attachable accessories include microphones and monitors.

Carrying methods

Cameras are delicate pieces of tech that cost more than average electronics, making it essential to use equipment that can carry cameras and accessories with full assurance of safety. There are many carrying options for a variety of camera usages, including over-the-shoulder bags, backpacks, and hard shell cases.
